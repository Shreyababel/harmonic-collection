Off the Beaten Path: NYC’s Underrated Best Baked Goods

Mah-Ze-Dahr Bakery (Greenwich Village)

Mah-Ze-Dahr Bakery in Greenwich Village offers a sleek, minimalist atmosphere that attracts locals looking for a quiet pastry and coffee experience. Known for its cheesecakes and signature Mah-Ze-Dahr Bars, this bakery focuses on sophisticated flavors using high-quality ingredients. With a mix of American classics and European-inspired treats, Mah-Ze-Dahr remains under the tourist radar, creating an intimate space for flavor and texture enthusiasts.

Sullivan Street Bakery

Sullivan Street Bakery in Hell's Kitchen delivers a rustic, neighborhood vibe, perfect for grabbing artisanal bread or a pastry without the Midtown rush. Famous for their stirato and sweet bomboloni, they bring traditional Italian craftsmanship to their baking. Known for their commitment to simple, high-quality ingredients and time-honored techniques, Sullivan Street remains an understated gem for bread lovers, offering a laid-back experience that keeps tourists at bay.

Two Little Red Hens

Two Little Red Hens on the Upper East Side is a cozy, homey spot hidden among the more elegant streets of the neighborhood. Famous for its cupcakes, pies, and cheesecakes, with the Brooklyn Blackout cake and New York-style cheesecake as standouts, this bakery masters traditional American classics. With a loyal local following, it’s a spot where New Yorkers go for perfectly executed baked goods without the trendy crowds.

Patisserie Chanson

Patisserie Chanson in Flatiron offers a chic yet relaxed atmosphere where French-inspired pastries are served with a modern twist. Known for their kouign-amann, éclairs, and delicate tarts, the bakery’s creations are both visually stunning and delicious, blending French tradition with contemporary flavors. This pastry shop offers a luxurious, laid-back escape from busier city spots, catering to those seeking high-quality desserts in a calm, non-touristy setting.

Ovenly

Ovenly in Brooklyn (Greenpoint and Park Slope) is a local favorite with a community-driven, laid-back vibe. Specializing in salty-sweet flavor combinations like their signature Salted Chocolate Chip Cookie and Blackout Cake, they also offer savory treats such as scones and focaccia. Their creative approach to flavor has garnered a loyal following, making Ovenly a Brooklyn staple that maintains its authenticity and charm, avoiding the tourist rush despite its growing popularity.

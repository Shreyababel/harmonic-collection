Ford Foundation Atrium Garden
Inside the Ford Foundation building, this indoor atrium is filled with tropical plants, trees, and flowers. Open to the public on weekdays, it provides a rare indoor green escape with a jungle-like vibe in the heart of the city.
Greenacre Park
Tucked away in Midtown, Greenacre Park is a small, tranquil space with lush greenery, a cascading waterfall, and café seating. It's an ideal escape for locals who want a break from the city's hustle without venturing far.
The Gardens at St. Luke in the Fields
A hidden gem in the West Village, this garden offers winding paths, benches, and a variety of plants, flowers, and trees. Maintained by the adjacent church, it's a serene space that feels miles away from NYC’s buzz.
Liz Christy Community Garden
Founded in the 1970s as the city’s first community garden, Liz Christy Community Garden is full of diverse plants, sculptures, and quiet sitting areas. This green oasis attracts locals who value its history and tranquil vibe.
Heather Garden
While Fort Tryon Park is relatively well-known, the Heather Garden remains a quieter, lesser-known section of it. Featuring hundreds of plants and flowers, it overlooks the Hudson River and provides locals with a stunning, peaceful escape.
Brooklyn Grange Rooftop Farms
Brooklyn Grange runs urban rooftop farms on multiple buildings in NYC. These farms grow organic vegetables and host events, while offering locals a peaceful retreat with panoramic city views and farm-fresh produce.
